Imperative Sentence

A type of sentence that gives advice or instructions or that expresses a request or command.

An imperative sentence typically begins with the base form of a verb. The implied subject you is said to be "understood".

An imperative sentence ends with a period or an exclamation point.

Examples

Sentence:

Dial me in to my conference.

Because our API will be made up of commands, we can safely assume that all imperative sentences will begin with an imperative mood, or the base form of a verb. 

Dial

This verb will be used to express a request or a command that can be mapped to one or many actions. Ok, great! We have our verb, but now what?

Using our verb we can create rules to determine what we should do with this command with the help of the remainder of our sentence.

In an imperative sentence there will be something that adds meaning to our verb. Either an adjective, adverb, or, in this case, a prepositional phrase 

in to my conference

We don't really care about the prepositional phrase, we care about the part that adds meaning to our verb. In this case it happens to be the object of the preposition 

conference

Once we find our verb, we are always going to search the sentence for a word that gives meaing to our verb and makes sense with our verb.

At this point we could search for further modifications; however, to simplify processing on our end I will assume our object of the verb is always going to be the person making the request. 

me
